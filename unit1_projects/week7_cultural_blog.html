Phase 0 Unit 3: Week 7


My Values


Adventure, Confidence, Control, Creativity, Discipline, Education, Faith, Family, Financial Security, Friends, Freedom, Fulfillment, Forgiveness, Fun, God, Growth, Happiness, Health, Hope, Honesty, Humor, Independence, Integrity, Kindness, Knowledge, Marriage, Peace of mind, Power, Progress, Reason, Security, Self-reliance, Service, Spirituality, Strength, Success, Truth, Wisdom


During the first week of Phase 0, I was asked to share my values. I've listed them above. This week I was asked to write about how I feel when I think about my values and if I think it can help mediate stereotype threat if I recognize I am in it. I've spoken briefly about my strategy to combat stereotype threat last week. I've stated that I will use a growth mindset to fight against anxiety, depression or anger.


This week I will write about using how I feel about my values to combat stereotype threat. Looking at my values above, I see a lot of positive things. Generally, I tend to be a very negative person. As I mentioned last week, our minds are constantly monitoring and interpreting everything that's happening around us. And that is very natural for human beings to do that.


However, problems arise when we interpret those things negatively and we have an exaggerated reaction. By thinking about my values, I can start using a new lens of which I can look for areas of learning and constructive action. I think values such as adventure, creativity, fun, growth, hope, knowledge, and reason will help me realign my trajectory towards progress.


This is pretty much a crazy experience and I hope to learn more about Engineering Empathy and Growth Mindset during my time at Dev Bootcamp.
